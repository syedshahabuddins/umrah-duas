Introduction

For the sincere Mehdavi believer, Umrah is more than a ritual; it is a homecoming of the soul. It is an opportunity to strip away worldly attachments, to walk in the footsteps of the Prophet (ﷺ) and the Imam of the Age, and to stand before Allah in a state of pure devotion.

This guide outlines the essential pillars (Arkan) of Umrah, infusing each step with the profound supplications from our tradition to ensure your journey is not only valid but spiritually transformative.

Pillars of Umrah

The validity and completion of your Umrah rest upon these essential acts:

Compulsory (Farz) Deeds

  1. Ihram: The sacred state of intention and purity
  2. Tawaf: Circling the House of Allah (Ka'bah)

After wearing Ihram, these become compulsory:

  • Niyyat (Intention)
  • Talbiyah

Mandatory (Wajib) Deeds

  1. Sa'i: The walk between Safa and Marwah
  2. Tahallul: Shaving or cutting hair after Tawaf and Sa'i

How to Use This Guide

This guide is designed to be used during your Umrah:

  • Each section covers one pillar of Umrah
  • Supplications are presented with Arabic text, Urdu transliteration, and translation
  • The design is optimized for mobile devices for easy reading during Umrah
  • Use the navigation buttons to move between sections
